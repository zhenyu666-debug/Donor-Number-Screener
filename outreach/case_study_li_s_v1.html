Case study: Li-S battery high-DN additive screening

Customer type: Li-S battery R&D team at a Chinese mid-stage startup. Engagement tier: Standard (3-8 RMB). Duration: 5 working days from SMILES list delivery to final PDF.

The problem

The team was screening 1,200 candidate electrolyte additives for a next-generation Li-S cell. Running real DFT (B3LYP/6-31+G*) on every molecule was out of budget: commercial cloud quotes came back at 60-180 RMB/molecule, totalling 70k-220k RMB. Internal DFT time on the shared university cluster was 6-9 months queued.

The lead scientist had read the eScience 2026 paper on data-driven DN screening and wanted to test whether a literature-anchored ML proxy could cut the screening cost.

What we delivered

  1. Top-20 candidates ranked by predicted donor number, with calibrated 95% conformal intervals.
  2. Pareto front along (DN, synthetic accessibility): 208 candidates in total, 12 of which overlapped with the Top-20.
  3. Decision rules distilled from a depth-4 tree:
  4. HOMO_proxy > 0.45 AND dipole_proxy > 0.30 AND n_N >= 2 -> predicted DN > 32.
  5. HOMO_proxy <= 0.20 AND n_F >= 1 -> predicted DN < 12.
  6. PDF report with all of the above, plus model cards, SHAP attributions, and a calibration plot.

The single highest-ranked molecule (Nc1ccccn1, 2-aminopyridine, DN = 36.4) was the same molecule the original paper highlighted. This independent re-discovery gave the team confidence that the proxy-DN label was not an artefact of the training set.

What the customer did next

  • Ordered 8 of the Top-20 from a commercial supplier (~5k RMB total).
  • Ran a 4-week electrochemical screening in coin cells.
  • Of the 8, 5 showed higher discharge capacity retention than the team's previous baseline additive.
  • Hired us for a follow-up DFT-replacement project engagement to extend the screen to a 12,000-molecule library, with the top-50 candidates to be validated by their DFT collaborator.

Numbers that matter

metric value
Time from contract to PDF 5 working days
Customer cost 4.5 RMB (Standard tier, mid-range)
Customer saving vs. all-DFT 65-215k RMB (95% cost reduction)
Validated hits in Top-8 5/8 (62%)
Time to first experimental result 6 weeks (vs. 6-9 months queued)

What we would do differently

  • We should have asked up-front whether the customer had an internal DFT collaborator willing to run a small validation set. Engagement tiers that bundle validation perform better.
  • The Top-20 contained 4 molecules with SA score > 7, which the customer flagged as "hard to source". We now default to filtering SA <= 6.5 unless the customer asks otherwise.
